# 🏥 Diabetes Patient Readmission Analysis

> Transforming healthcare data into actionable insights using Python and Tableau.

## 📌 Project Overview

Hospital readmissions are a major challenge in the healthcare industry, leading to increased treatment costs and placing additional pressure on hospital resources. Understanding the factors that contribute to patient readmission can help healthcare providers improve patient care and reduce avoidable hospital visits.

This project analyzes over **101,000 diabetic patient records** collected from **130 U.S. hospitals (1999–2008)**. The dataset was cleaned and analyzed using Python, and an interactive Tableau dashboard was developed to identify patterns and trends associated with hospital readmissions.

---

## 🎯 Project Objectives

- Analyze diabetic patient readmission patterns.
- Identify demographic factors affecting readmission.
- Explore the relationship between hospital stay duration and readmission.
- Compare readmission rates across gender.
- Create an interactive Tableau dashboard for healthcare insights.
- Provide recommendations that could help reduce hospital readmissions.

---

## 📊 Dataset Information

**Dataset Name:** Diabetes 130-US Hospitals for Years 1999–2008

**Source:** UCI Machine Learning Repository

**Number of Records:** 101,766

**Hospitals Included:** 130 U.S. Hospitals

**Time Period:** 1999–2008

The dataset contains demographic, clinical, and hospital encounter information for diabetic patients, making it suitable for analyzing factors related to hospital readmission.

## 📊 Key Insights

### 1. Readmission Distribution

The majority of diabetic patients were **not readmitted**, while a smaller percentage returned within 30 days or after 30 days.

---

### 2. Gender Analysis

Both male and female patients experienced readmissions, with female patients representing a slightly larger proportion due to the overall patient distribution.

---

### 3. Hospital Stay Analysis

Patients who were readmitted within 30 days generally had longer hospital stays compared to patients who were not readmitted.

---

### 4. Emergency Visit Analysis

Patients with frequent emergency visits showed a greater likelihood of hospital readmission, indicating that emergency care utilization may be associated with higher readmission risk.

---

## 💡 Business Insights

The analysis suggests that healthcare providers can improve patient outcomes by focusing on patients who exhibit characteristics associated with higher readmission rates.

Potential strategies include:

- Enhanced discharge planning
- Better post-discharge follow-up
- Monitoring patients with longer hospital stays
- Early intervention for high-risk diabetic patients
